### American Politics

An introduction to the institutions, processes, and actors that shape American national politics, including Congress, the presidency, the courts, political parties, interest groups, elections, and public opinion.

### State and Local Government

An examination of subnational politics in the United States, covering state legislatures, governors, courts, local governments, intergovernmental relations, and state and local public policy.

### Quantitative Analysis

An applied introduction to statistical methods for political and social science research. Topics include research design, descriptive statistics, regression analysis, and causal inference.

### Graduate Quantitative Analysis

A graduate-level methods course covering research design, statistical inference, regression, and applied data analysis for students in political science and public administration.

### Public Policy

An examination of the policy-making process, policy analysis, and the role of government in designing and implementing public programs. Topics include agenda setting, legislative and executive decision-making, bureaucratic politics, and policy evaluation.

### Introduction to Public Administration

An undergraduate survey of public administration, covering bureaucratic theory, organizational behavior, human resources, budgeting, and public sector ethics.

### Graduate Public Budgeting and Finance

A graduate-level course on the theory and practice of public budgeting, fiscal policy, revenue systems, and financial management in government.

### Graduate Public Policy

A graduate-level survey of public policy theory and practice, covering policy design, implementation, evaluation, and the political economy of public sector reform.

### Political Science Capstone: Inequality in the United States

A capstone seminar examining economic and political inequality in the United States, integrating students' political science training through independent research projects.
